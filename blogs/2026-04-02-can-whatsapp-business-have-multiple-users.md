<p>WhatsApp Business makes it easy for small teams to talk to customers in real time. But in larger organizations, it’s a struggle to manage all those messages from a single phone. You start off with one person managing messages, then need to add access for another person. And then another.</p>



<p>Running a business with just one WhatsApp phone number feels like trying to answer every customer call from a single landline. It’s clunky, slow, and completely unrealistic.</p>



<p>If you’re trying to provide fast, reliable customer support but your team is passing around one phone or locked out of conversations entirely, you’re not alone.</p>



<p>If you’ve hit that point, you’re probably wondering: can <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/how-use-whatsapp-business-company-everything-you-need-know/">WhatsApp Business</a> support multiple users?</p>



<p>The answer is yes. There are two ways to use WhatsApp business. You can use the app and link multiple devices to one account, or use the WhatsApp Business Platform (API), which is designed for multiple users to manage conversations at the same time.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>WhatsApp Business for multiple users</strong></h2>



<p>The free WhatsApp Business app that many companies start out with is designed for very small businesses with no more than five employees. But once your WhatsApp channel grows, it doesn’t take long before it turns into a bottleneck for the customer’s experience.</p>



<p>With WhatsApp becoming more popular, this won’t take too long. According to our 2025 Sinch report, The state of customer communications, 15% of Gen Z prefer to use messaging apps like WhatsApp for support conversations. And 40% of consumers of all ages say that they use OTT channels like WhatsApp to interact with businesses.</p>



<p>That’s why being stuck passing a phone around with the same WhatsApp business number will get very cumbersome very fast. All it takes is one person out sick or out of reach, and suddenly no one can respond to a customer inquiry.</p>



<p>Passing around the “WhatsApp phone” between team members becomes a logistical nightmare. Your inbox becomes chaotic, WhatsApp messages pile up, response times suffer, and customers grow frustrated waiting for answers.</p>



<p>That’s why WhatsApp introduced a multi-device feature for the business app. With this setup, you can connect up to four additional devices to the same WhatsApp Business account. Everyone can access the same inbox from their own device, so they no longer have to share phones or chase down replies.</p>



<p>Let’s consider a direct-to-consumer (DTC) brand selling skincare across the U.S. Their support team works across time zones from New York to California, but only the New York team has the WhatsApp phone. By the time their day starts, they’ve missed dozens of messages and customer inquiries that came in later in the evening from the West Coast.</p>



<p>Or let’s say a local radio station in the UK uses WhatsApp to interact with listeners, run audience competitions, and manage donations. Even with everyone working in the same office, passing one phone around departments to handle all these messages turns into an organizational disaster.</p>



<p>That’s where many turn to WhatsApp’s multi-device feature for the business app. While multi-device access helps slightly, you can’t solve the real problems of lack of routing, visibility, and automation. Let’s explore why.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to use WhatsApp Business on two devices</strong></h2>



<p>The multi-device setup process for the WhatsApp Business app is identical to the standard <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/standard-whatsapp-vs-whatsapp-business-accounts/">WhatsApp messenger app</a> you use to talk to your friends and family. Your primary device must be a smartphone, but you can connect laptops, desktops, tablets, and additional phones to access the same business conversations through <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/whatsapp-business-web/">WhatsApp Web or Desktop</a>.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to add multiple users on the WhatsApp Business app</strong></h2>



<p>Adding multiple users to the WhatsApp Business app is a fairly simple process and takes only a few minutes. On the WhatsApp Business app, you can use a maximum of five devices—one mobile phone and four additional devices (PC/ mobile/ tablet) with the same phone number.</p>



<p>Let’s examine how to <a href="https://faq.whatsapp.com/647349420360876">add multiple users/devices on WhatsApp settings</a>. This process is easy and takes only a few minutes on an iPhone or any Android device.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Go to your WhatsApp Business app on your primary phone</li>



<li>Tap the icon with three vertical dots</li>



<li>Click “Linked devices”</li>



<li>Click on “Link a device”</li>



<li>Scan the QR code, and you’re done</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The WhatsApp Business Platform for multiple users</strong></h2>



<p>When message volume is low and responsibilities can easily be shared, having multiple users makes a lot of sense. But as you scale, so might the number of users you need. Plus, things can get complicated very quickly. Two team members might respond to the same message at the same time, confusing the customer on the other end. A conversation might get missed because it isn’t clear whose responsibility it is to respond. Or someone did respond, but it’s not clear who.</p>



<p>These types of scenarios are typically what makes teams realize they’ve hit the limits for what multiple users on WhatsApp Business can do for them. It’s when you need more coordination to make your business work.</p>



<p>Luckily, WhatsApp provides a solution for assigning conversations, managing workflows, and tracking activity across users.</p>

<p>The result? Fewer missed connections, higher customer engagement, and lower support costs.</p>
