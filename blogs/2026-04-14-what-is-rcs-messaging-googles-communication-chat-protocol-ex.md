<p>If SMS had been invented today, it probably wouldn&#8217;t be limited to 160 characters of plain text, and you wouldn&#8217;t need MMS just to send a picture. Instead, you&#8217;d probably end up with something a lot closer to Rich Communication Services (RCS), a messaging protocol built for the smartphone era with rich media, tappable buttons, branded messages for business messaging, and more.</p>



<p>So, what is RCS messaging, and why is it taking off now? RCS has been around for a while (since 2006, actually), but it&#8217;s now hitting its stride. With support from Android, carriers worldwide, and even Apple support in some markets, RCS is living up to its promise: making messaging smarter, more secure, and way more interactive.</p>



<p>So, what does that mean for your business? Better engagement and sales, more trust, and conversations that actually drive action – all in your customers&#8217; native messaging inboxes.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What is an RCS message?</h2>



<p>What does RCS mean? RCS stands for Rich Communication Services – a messaging protocol developed by the <a href="https://www.gsma.com/solutions-and-impact/technologies/networks/gsma_resources/rich-communication-services/">Global System for Mobile Communications Association (GSMA)</a> as the evolution of SMS. An RCS text message is more than just a text – it&#8217;s an interactive, app-like experience delivered directly to your customer&#8217;s native messaging inbox.</p>



<p>An RCS message supports rich chat features like high-res images and videos, rich cards, suggested replies, branded sender information, and real-time indicators like &#8220;typing…&#8221; and &#8220;read.&#8221; Best of all, RCS messaging delivers these interactive experiences directly to a user&#8217;s native mobile messaging inbox on Android and Apple devices, meaning there&#8217;s no third-party app required.</p>


    <div class="callout text-body-color px-5 py-6 px-md-6 px-lg-7 py-md-7 longform-spacings rounded-lg bg-primary">

        <div class="content-body"> <p>So what is an RCS chat, exactly? An RCS chat is a conversation that takes place using the RCS protocol rather than traditional SMS. RCS chats offer communication capabilities like read receipts, typing indicators, and high-quality media sharing – and can also include multimedia like web pages, maps, and other images, all directly in a user&#8217;s native mobile inbox.</p></div>
    </div>



<p>For users, RCS means better conversations. For businesses, RCS means you can deliver sleek, branded, app-like experiences straight to your customers&#8217; native text inboxes. Think order confirmations with maps, product carousels, or even full two-way conversations with customer support.</p>



<p>And because businesses can struggle with getting users to download and regularly use their mobile apps, RCS can help reduce that friction by bringing app-like functionality directly into the messaging experience. Want to understand the full picture of how messages flow? Check out our deep-dive on <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/how-rcs-works/">how RCS works</a>.</p>



<p>In 2025, RCS had over one and a half billion monthly active users, and its reach is still growing in 2026, thanks to expanding support by Apple and mobile operators.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Is RCS chat only for Android phones? Does Apple have RCS messaging?</h2>



<p>For years, RCS was largely an Android experience. That shifted in 2024 when Apple introduced <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/rcs-ios-18/">RCS support in iOS 18</a> for person-to-person (P2P) messaging, starting in select markets with carriers like AT&amp;T, T-Mobile, and Verizon.</p>



<p>Apple has continued to widen their support and eventually began rolling out the application-to-person (A2P) version of RCS, known as RCS for Business (formerly &#8220;RBM&#8221;) with certain operators.</p>



<p>In 2025, Apple rolled out iOS 26, which brought broader updates that shape how people receive messages and calls. Features like filtering unknown senders, a dedicated SMS spam folder, and priority handling for time-sensitive messages give users more control over what they see.</p>



<p>Apple also confirmed support for <a href="https://www.gsma.com/solutions-and-impact/technologies/networks/gsma_resources/rich-communication-services/">RCS Universal Profile 3.0</a>, the industry standard for RCS that standardizes RCS features across devices. When it happens, it will bring features like in-line replies, editing, unsending, and end-to-end, cross-platform encryption between Android and iPhone. However, this update is not yet available in iOS 26.</p>



<p>The rollout continues to move quickly. In March 2026, Apple released iOS 26.4, which includes testing for <a href="https://9to5mac.com/2026/03/18/apple-releases-ios-26-4-rc-for-iphone-with-upcoming-features/">end-to-end encrypted RCS messaging</a> – a significant step toward the cross-platform encryption that the GSMA&#8217;s RCS Universal Profile 3.0 has been working toward. This signals that full, interoperable E2EE between iPhone and Android is getting closer to reality.</p>



<p>In short, RCS is no longer an Android-only channel. As Apple expands support and more carriers adopt RCS messaging, more and more businesses will be able to reach customers with messaging that&#8217;s more interactive, trusted, and effective – no matter what phone they use.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Where is RCS messaging used?</h2>



<p>RCS has full coverage in Canada, Mexico, Brazil, the U.K., Germany, France, Spain, Italy, and India. Other countries offer full support via Google&#8217;s RCS services or partial support, with a selection of local mobile operators providing RCS.</p>



<p>When it comes to RCS for Business, coverage depends on a mix of mobile carrier support and regional rollout, especially as Apple gradually expands availability.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">RCS vs. SMS, MMS, and OTT messaging apps</h2>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="" class="wp-image-184303" height="1024" src="https://sinch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/SI-Handset-Message-Type-01-505x1024.png" width="505" /></figure>



<p>To understand the differences between an RCS message and text messaging, it might be helpful to first understand the differences between <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/sms-vs-mms/">MMS and SMS</a>.</p>



<p>While SMS is simple and reliable, it&#8217;s limited to 160 characters per message. MMS messages add the ability to send multimedia attachments, but RCS offers a much more interactive user experience.</p>



<p>And that&#8217;s because RCS messages work with other elements of a smartphone, like a web browser or maps, to create way more engaging conversations.</p>



<p>Is RCS going to replace traditional SMS? Not exactly. RCS runs on a mobile data connection or Wi-Fi, meaning SMS will still kick in when there&#8217;s no internet – just like iMessage will <a href="https://sinch.com/messaging/conversation-api/channel-priority-fallback/">fall back</a> to SMS when needed.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">RCS vs. SMS</h3>



<p>SMS is simple and reliable, but limited to 160 characters per message with no support for rich media, read receipts, or typing indicators. RCS removes all of those limitations, delivering an interactive, app-like experience directly in the native messaging inbox – no download required.</p>



<p>RCS runs on mobile data or Wi-Fi, so SMS will still kick in as a fallback when there&#8217;s no internet connection.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Exciting RCS features that set it apart</h2>



<p>What does RCS do better than SMS? Well, just about everything! To see the full range of what&#8217;s possible, explore the different <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/rcs-business-message-types/">types of RCS for Business messages</a>. Where SMS is limited to plain text and links, RCS offers features like:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Better security:</strong> <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/rcs-security/">RCS messages are encrypted</a> in transit, and in some cases, like RCS chats in Google Messages, they&#8217;re protected with end-to-end encryption.</li>



<li><strong>Higher text limits:</strong> SMS messages are limited to 160 characters, while text portions of RCS messages are limited to 3,072 characters.</li>



<li><strong>High-resolution images and videos:</strong> Grab attention with gorgeous high-resolution photos and videos, GIFs, carousels, and other dynamic features right in the native messaging app.</li>



<li><strong>Larger file sizes:</strong> Send images and videos up to 100MB (though Google recommends images up to 2MB and videos up to 10MB for the best experience).</li>



<li><strong>Message reactions and typing indicators:</strong> Just like in modern messaging apps, RCS supports reactions (like thumbs up or hearts) and shows when someone is typing. These features are available on both Android and iOS, and for cross-device messaging.</li>



<li><strong>Read receipts:</strong> If the recipient has their read receipts enabled, you&#8217;ll see the word &#8220;Read&#8221; along with the time they read your RCS message.</li>



<li><strong>Cross-app connectivity:</strong> Send messages that open in browsers, maps, and other applications.</li>



<li><strong>Easy, one-tap replies:</strong> RCS for Business messages make it easy to reply to messages with one tap with suggested replies.</li>



<li><strong>Group chats:</strong> Connect securely in group messages with friends, family, and team members without requiring another chat app.</li>



<li><strong>Google Wallet integration:</strong> Provides a quicker and easier way to access everyday essentials.</li>
</ul>

<p>Once you&#8217;re verified and live, here&#8217;s what businesses love most about using RCS:</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Enhanced branding and trust</h3>



<p>In our <a href="https://sinch.com/resources/state-of-rcs-customer-communications/">2025 State of RCS in customer communications</a> report, 79% of consumers said logos and verification checkmarks make messages feel more trustworthy.</p>



<p>RCS messages display your business name, logo, and <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/rcs-verified-senders/">verified sender</a> status so customers always know exactly who they&#8217;re talking to – making it ideal for sensitive messages like <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/rcs-otp/">one-time passwords sent via RCS</a>.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Boosted read and response rates</h3>



<p>Because RCS messages land in the native messaging inbox with rich, interactive content, they consistently outperform SMS on open rates, read rates, and response rates.</p>



<p>Our <a href="https://sinch.com/state-of-customer-communications/">2025 State of customer communications</a> research shows that 40% of business leaders believe RCS will increase customer engagement through its interactive features.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Elevated analytics and insights</h3>



<p>Handset delivery receipts, open rates, and read rates give you more in-depth information about the status of messages. This makes A/B testing and campaign optimization easier – and more effective.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">No extra downloads</h3>



<p>All new Android devices have built-in RCS, so messages from verified businesses can go directly to the native messaging inbox instead of needing a separate app.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Rich, interactive features</h3>



<p>From image carousels and videos to reply buttons and suggested actions, you can keep users engaged – and guide them toward the next step – without sending them elsewhere.</p>



<p>Explore the full range of <a href="https://sinch.com/blog/rcs-business-message-types/">RCS for Business message types</a> to see what&#8217;s possible.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">RCS chatbot best practices we learned from customers that use RCS for Business</h2>



<p>One of the most powerful applications of RCS for Business is the chatbot experience – delivering automated, conversational interactions that feel personal and on-brand. Here&#8217;s how to do it right:</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Be clear about what your RCS chatbot does</h3>



<p>Set expectations upfront. Let customers know they&#8217;re interacting with an automated experience, what it can help with, and what topics are in scope. Clear framing reduces frustration and builds trust.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Provide an optimal experience</h3>



<p>Use RCS&#8217;s rich features – suggested replies, carousels, quick-tap buttons – to make conversations as frictionless as possible. The easier it is for customers to take action, the better your results will be.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Always have a human fallback option</h3>



<p>No chatbot can handle every situation. Always provide a clear path to a live agent, whether that&#8217;s a &#8220;Talk to a person&#8221; suggested reply or a handoff trigger when the bot reaches its limits.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Provide suggestions to steer users</h3>



<p>Don&#8217;t leave customers staring at a blank text field. Suggested replies and action buttons guide users through the conversation and make it far more likely they&#8217;ll complete the intended action.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Ensure your chatbot reflects your brand</h3>



<p>Your RCS chatbot is a brand touchpoint. The tone, language, and visual elements should all align with your wider brand identity.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Does RCS cost money?</h2>



<p>For consumers, RCS messaging is free to use – it works over Wi-Fi or your mobile data connection rather than consuming SMS credits. However, it does require an active data connection, so if you&#8217;re on a limited data plan, RCS messages will use a small amount of data (though typically far less than browsing the web or streaming video).</p>



<p>For businesses using <a href="https://sinch.com/pricing/rcs/">RCS for Business (A2P messaging), costs</a> are similar to SMS in that you pay per message sent, though pricing varies by provider and region.</p>



<p>The good news: because RCS messages are more engaging and drive higher conversion rates, the ROI typically far exceeds that of SMS.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to enable RCS messaging on your smartphone to experience it for yourself</h2>



<p>Getting started with RCS is straightforward on both Android and iPhone.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">How to enable RCS messaging on Android</h3>



<p>All new Android devices come with RCS chat built in. Here&#8217;s how to turn it on in <a href="https://messages.google.com/">Google Messages</a>:</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>Open Google Messages on your device.</li>



<li>Tap your profile picture in the top right, then select <strong>Message settings</strong>.</li>



<li>Tap <strong>RCS chats</strong>.</li>



<li>Toggle <strong>RCS chats</strong> on.</li>
</ol>



<p>Note that you may need to verify your phone number before RCS is enabled.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">How to enable RCS messaging on iPhone</h3>



<p>If you have iOS 18 or higher and your carrier supports RCS on iPhone, you can enable it in your iPhone&#8217;s Message settings:</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>Go to <strong>Settings &gt; Apps &gt; Messages</strong>.</li>



<li>Tap <strong>RCS messaging</strong>.</li>



<li>Toggle it on.</li>
</ol>



<p>Once enabled, you&#8217;ll see &#8220;Text Message • RCS&#8221; in the message field when composing a message to an Android user, confirming RCS is active.</p>
